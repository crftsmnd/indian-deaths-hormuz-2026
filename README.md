# Indian Deaths at Strait of Hormuz Due to US Attacks (June 2026)

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Status:** Ongoing situation
**Sources:** BBC Verify, The Guardian, Al Jazeera, Times of India, NDTV

---

## Executive Summary

Three Indian sailors were killed and dozens more endangered when the US military struck three commercial vessels in the Strait of Hormuz over a 72-hour period in June 2026. The attacks were part of the US naval blockade of Iranian ports, which began on April 13, 2026, after Iran closed the strait — a chokepoint through which ~20% of the world's oil and gas supplies transit.

The killings have severely strained India-US relations, sparked domestic political outcry in India, and drawn condemnation from the International Maritime Organization.

## India's Diplomatic Response

1. **Summoned US diplomat** — Deputy Chief of Mission Jason Meeks received a formal demarche at the Ministry of External Affairs
2. **Ministerial statement** — Shipping Minister Sarbananda Sonowal confirmed deaths, ordered repatriation: *"It is deeply unfortunate... I have directed officials to ensure immediate repatriation of rescued crew and swift return of mortal remains."*
3. **EAM Jaishankar** spoke directly to US Secretary of State Marco Rubio — stated lethal actions against commercial shipping "are not justified"
4. **MEA spokesperson Randhir Jaiswal**: *"These attacks must cease and end. We also call for dialogue and diplomacy."*
5. **At G7 Summit** — Modi and Trump met in France with the deaths as a central agenda item
6. **India lodged formal protest** against the targeting of commercial shipping

---

## US Response

- **CENTCOM** confirmed all strikes and justified them under the blockade mandate
- **Secretary of State Marco Rubio** refused to apologize or offer condolences: *"Violations of the US blockade and the illicit transport of Iranian oil will not be tolerated."*
- **No US acknowledgment of casualties** in initial statement — only confirmed a strike on an "uncooperative" vessel
- The US State Department readout made no mention of Indian deaths

## Domestic Political Fallout in India

- **Rahul Gandhi** (opposition leader): Accused Modi of being *"silent... Foreign powers kill our citizens. Our government quietly obeys orders like an obedient servant."*
- **Shashi Tharoor** (opposition MP, External Affairs Committee chair): *"How can a 'friend' and strategic partner be so deeply insensitive? Practically every merchant ship has Indian crew. Are they all fair game for US missiles now?"*
- **Manoj Yadav** (FSUI General Secretary): *"I am 101% certain US forces knew exactly how many Indians were on those vessels. Detaining them was a viable alternative."*
- **Media headlines**: "No apology from Washington"

## Seafarer Safety Concerns

- **~300,000** Indian seafarers serve on international vessels — one of the largest maritime workforces globally
- **~18,000** Indian seafarers currently in the Gulf region
- **13 Indian-flagged vessels** remain stranded in the Strait of Hormuz
- **FSUI statement**: *"Seafarers are workers. They are not soldiers. The international community cannot remain a silent spectator."*
- **IMO Secretary-General Arsenio Dominguez**: Strongly condemned *"any act from any party that endangers the lives of seafarers"*
- Seafarers' unions called for better protection and safe passage for commercial vessels

## Geopolitical Context

- **US blockade** of Iranian ports began April 13, 2026, after Iran closed the Strait of Hormuz
- CENTCOM reports: 9 vessels disabled, 135+ redirected since blockade began
- US-Israel war on Iran ongoing; Pakistani-brokered ceasefire (April) remains fragile
- Iran has selectively allowed "friendly" nations' ships to pass
- India-US relations at lowest ebb in years — tariffs, trust deficit, Trump's closeness to Pakistan
- US foreign policy analyst Derek J Grossman: *"Rubio's words were very tone deaf. Some 'friend' Trump's America is to India."*
