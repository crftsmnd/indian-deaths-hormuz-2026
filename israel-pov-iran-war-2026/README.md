# Israel's Perspective on the 2026 Iran War

**Date:** June 22, 2026
**Research Scope:** Israeli strategic objectives, military operations, domestic politics, public opinion, and endgame calculus regarding the 2026 war against Iran.

---

## 1. Strategic Objectives

### Primary: Regime Change

Israel's stated war aim, repeated consistently by Prime Minister Benjamin Netanyahu since Day 1, is **regime change in Tehran**. At the war's outset on February 28, Netanyahu declared the US-Israeli attack *"will create the conditions for the brave Iranian people to rid themselves of the yoke of tyranny."* He later addressed Iranians directly on X: *"Be ready to seize the moment!"*

This is a maximalist position. Behind closed doors, Israeli analysts acknowledge a more realistic spectrum of acceptable outcomes.

### Minimum Acceptable Outcome

Per retired Col. Miri Eisen (former IDF Military Intelligence), Netanyahu would likely settle for *"a heavily diminished Iran"* — where:

- The **nuclear program** is reduced to an irrecoverable level
- **Ballistic missile arsenals** and launch infrastructure are largely destroyed
- Iran's **regional proxy network** (Hezbollah, Hamas, Houthis) is severed from Iranian command

Retired Brig. Gen. Yossi Kuperwasser (Jerusalem Institute for Strategy and Security) frames it as: *"Our dream is that the regime in Iran is going to change and be replaced by a regime that is pro-Western, more or less ready... to give up the ideas of hegemony in the Middle East."* He then adds: *"That is our dream. But after the dreams comes reality."*

### The Nuclear Imperative

Iran retains approximately **450 kg of highly enriched uranium** in underground tunnel facilities after the June 2025 Twelve-Day War. Israel's assessment was that waiting would allow Iran to rebuild its nuclear and missile capabilities. According to Brig. Gen. Yossi Kuperwasser: *"We caused the nuclear project very devastating damage [in June 2025], but we didn't finish the job."*

Israeli National Security Adviser Eyal Hulata frames the fallback objective: if regime change is abandoned, Israel would pursue goals easier to *"define and also measure"* — primarily further degrading Iran's missile and nuclear programs.

Adi Stoler (IDF Spokesperson's Office): Phase 2 of the war targets Iran's remaining nuclear capabilities while continuing to deplete missile arsenals and assassinate regime officials. *"The goal is to make sure that Iran will never pose a threat again to the State of Israel."*

## 2. Military Operations

### Opening Salvo

The joint US-Israeli campaign — **Operation Epic Fury** (US) / **Operation Lion's Roar** (Israel) — began February 28, 2026 with coordinated airstrikes across Iran targeting:

- Missile storage facilities and launch infrastructure
- Military bases (Parchin, etc.)
- IRGC command centers and intelligence institutions
- Nuclear program sites (Atomic Energy Organization of Iran)
- Ministry of Defense and Ministry of Intelligence buildings

The assassination of **Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei** occurred during the opening phase — a dramatic escalation that transformed the military campaign into a political war for regime survival. Rather than triggering collapse, it hardened Iranian resistance and triggered nationwide mobilization.

### Iran's Asymmetric Response

Iran retaliated with sustained ballistic missile and drone barrages targeting Israeli territory. Israel's multi-layered defense system (Iron Dome → David's Sling → Arrow) has intercepted many projectiles, but the sustained onslaught creates a significant logistical challenge — each interception consumes expensive interceptors, and prolonged attacks risk stockpile depletion.

**Hezbollah** on Israel's northern border has fired into northern Israel, forcing civilians into bomb shelters. The war has expanded into a multi-front conflict for Israel, with active theaters in:
- **Iran** — long-range strikes
- **Lebanon** — Hezbollah rocket and ground attacks
- **Yemen** — Houthi missile attacks toward Israeli-linked shipping
- **Strait of Hormuz** — broader maritime war affecting global shipping

## 3. Domestic Politics & Public Opinion

### Overwhelming Public Support — With a Catch

An **Israel Democracy Institute poll** found **93% of Jewish Israelis support the war**, with most believing it should continue until the regime is overthrown. According to Iranian-born lecturer Meir Javedanfar (Reichman University): *"Most Israelis believe that this war is necessary in order to seriously degrade the regime's military capabilities and also at least try to bring down the regime."*

However, Israelis want assurance this is the elusive *"last round"* — the war that finally neutralizes the existential threat Iran has posed for decades. There is little appetite for an endless, inconclusive campaign.

### Netanyahu's Political Calculus

For Netanyahu, the stakes are existential — not just for Israel but for his political survival. Still damaged by the **October 7, 2023 Hamas attack**, he needs a decisive victory. Anything less than regime change risks being perceived as failure by the Israeli public.

His messaging has been fixed on regime change to manage domestic expectations. But as retired IDF planner Shlomo Brom notes: *"An exit strategy needs to be realistic. Because, if it is not realistic, there is no exit."*

### US-Israel Tensions Over Strategy

The Carnegie Endowment reports growing divergence between US and Israeli goals as the war has progressed:

- **Trump** — increasingly focused on oil flow and a quick exit. His Venezuela model: find a pragmatic Iranian faction to negotiate with. He's on a tighter timetable with shorter patience.
- **Netanyahu** — wants to see the regime significantly diminished or gone. Less flexible on exit conditions.

According to retired Col. Miri Eisen: *"In most of our wars, it takes an outer force to stop us."* The implication: America will ultimately call when the war ends, and Israel will have to accept it.

The **Ipsos poll (March 2026)** found Americans hold negative views of the strikes. A **IMEU Policy Project poll** found 56% of Americans believe the war benefits Israel more than the US.

## 4. Endgame Scenarios

### Scenario A — Regime Change (Israel's Dream)

The Iranian people rise up, the Islamic Republic falls, replaced by a pro-Western government that abandons the nuclear program, ballistic missiles, and proxy networks. Netanyahu declares total victory.

**Reality check:** No evidence of mass uprising. The regime chose hard-liner **Mojtaba Khamenei** (wounded in the war) to replace his slain father. Per Shlomo Brom: *"Judging from the little information that is coming from Iran, I don't see any movement toward regime change."*

### Scenario B — Diminished Iran (Likely Compromise)

Iran's nuclear and military capabilities are severely degraded, its proxy network disrupted, but the regime survives. Israel declares victory based on concrete military achievements. Trump declares victory and exits. Netanyahu faces domestic questions about whether this was enough.

### Scenario C — Stalematt / Attrition

Neither side achieves decisive victory. Iran continues asymmetric attacks (missiles, drones, proxies). Israel and the US struggle to find an exit. Shlomo Brom warns the war could *"drag on"* until Trump *"will be fed up with it."*

**Observers note:** The war already ended on the Wikipedia infobox — listed as running **February 28 – June 17, 2026 (~3.5 months)**, with the result listed as "Strategic Iranian victory" (disputed). This suggests ceasefire talks (the Islamabad Memorandum) may have taken effect.

## 5. Broader Israeli Concerns

### Seafarer Safety

The Hormuz crisis (which killed 3 Indian sailors) reflects the broader maritime dimension that directly affects Israeli interests. Iran threatens the strait through which ~20% of global oil transits. Israel has been hit economically by energy price spikes.

### Hezbollah and the Northern Front

The war on Iran has reactivated Hezbollah on Israel's northern border. Israeli forces have conducted operations in southern Lebanon, including the Battle of Khiam, Battle of Bint Jbeil, and crossing the Litani River. A separate Israel–Lebanon ceasefire is under negotiation.

### ICC Scrutiny

Netanyahu faces potential International Criminal Court scrutiny over the conduct of the war, adding legal pressure to the political and military dimensions.
